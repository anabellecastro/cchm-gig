Troy Hankins

3D Modeler, 2D Animator, 3D Animator

I was the 3D content team lead for the Spring 2020 Senior Seminar class. My goal as team lead was to offer guidance and give assistance to my teammates as needed, while also encouraging collaborative work throughout the class. This year, we used programs such as, Maya, Substance Painter, and Photoshop to create high quality, digital 3D models for the Clark County Historical Museum. I am very pleased with the content that we have created, and I am grateful for all of the knowledge and skill I have gained throughout my time in the DTC program.

Evan Astrue

3D Modeler, 3D Animator, Illustrator

View Portfolio

For my contribution to Project Renovare, I handled modeling 3D renders of Museum artifacts. Primarily, I used Autodesk Maya to sculpt the shape of the model, as well as Photoshop and Substance Painter to construct the textures for each model. Overall, I made sure to recreate models with close accuracy, and textures that best reflected the models in question to really help them come alive.

Cody Tucker

Game Designer, Level Designer, 3D Modeler

View Portfolio

I'm Cody Tucker and I specialize in 3D Art and Game Design from Washington State University Vancouver. I hard modeled four of the artifacts for the CCHM virtual tour: the Ogden pin, League of Women's Voters pin, the shipyard bracelet, and the Eisenhower hat and it's components. This process involved interpreting models from the direct source, creating textures to mimic the look and feel of the original artifact, and making them readily available to function on the website proper.
